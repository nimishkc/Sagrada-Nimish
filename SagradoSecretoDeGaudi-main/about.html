Our Vision

We seek to provide the users, based on our audience and technical research, with access to engaging and informative content regarding the cathedral’s history and architecture. Our interactive experiences have been designed and developed as a means of containing this content all in one place, in a way that can be viewed from the comfort of any device–whether through a desktop computer or mobile phone. It is an experience that has, until now, yet to have been contained in a single online environment, and we thus believe our website has the potential to present tourists and other users interested in the cathedral with significant value. We further strive to make this entire website experience accessible, having learned the importance of accessibility from our Authoring for Digital Media module, just as much as we have been driven to make it both fascinating and convenient for you and all of our users through the excessive research conducted for the development of this experience.

Our Project

Part One

The Concept

With the goal of constructing a building-based website for our shared study program, we decided on the Basílica de la Sagrada Família–a structure still experiencing an ongoing construction of its own. We found this as a unique opportunity and recognized the value in containing its significant history and architecture all in one place online, just in time to honour the cathedral’s timely completion–aiming for 100 years after Gaudí’s death in 1926.

Given our newly solidified concept, we divided the managerial roles based on our personal strengths and interests, and set out to work on this very website, hoping that our final project would further encourage personal contributions towards the final structure of the cathedral and make Gaudí’s vision a complete reality in the real world too.

Part Two

The Design

The initial idea brought about a need for a comprehensive design, rooted in audience and technical research. We discussed the users of our website in great depth and worked hard towards designing the overall structure of the online environment, including the main architectural view and interactive timeline that would allow our users to experience the Basílica like never before in an online context.

Upon analysing our target audience as a means of producing a user-centric design, we began outlining the main structure through flowcharts and wireframes, even selecting its main colours, ultimately deciding on the look and feel of our website that was ready to be filled in with content that you, our users, would have the joy of experiencing.

Part Three

The Development

With the outline of our website established, we began constructing our HTML, adding CSS, and conducting even more research on the Basílica for selecting only the most engaging and informative content for our website. You may discover this as both a visual and written narrative, allowing you to effectively navigate our website and explore our set of interactive experiences, specially crafted for you in relation to the Basílica.

Our efforts until this point have led to what is now the basis of our website–a place in which our two most beloved experiences developed for you, our users, could thoroughly shine as elegantly as the central towers of the Basílica itself. Making progress with the structure and writing of our content, we were led to the establishing of interactivity throughout our website and the main experiences for our users.

Part Four

The Interactivity

With our main content and structure of our website essentially developed, we needed a means of showcasing our main experiences using interactivity. Our architectural view and interactive timeline required a significant amount of additional groundwork in the form of back-end development, in order to develop the complete experiences you see here today.

As a part of the architectural view, a 3D model of the Basílica has been split into sections and colour-coded, then implemented using Javascript and ReactJS. You can find the current version of the model in the architectural view, where you can rotate and click specific sections to read more about each part of the Basílica. Try it out for yourself!

Part Five

The Accessibility

Our Web Authoring course at IDM emphasises the importance of web accessibility–and for good reason. As students of the program, we are driven to incorporate these matters into our own website. From the main accessibility button that allows you to make adjustments according to your personal needs, down to the modifications panel of the 3D model itself, everything you see on our website has gone through rigorous testing and careful accessibility considerations–including each and every element of the HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

We want to assure you that by using our website, you will not only have an engaging and informative experience, but also a highly accessible and customisable one that is unique to you and your needs, no matter the concerns you may bring. Accessibility has been made a primary concern from the very beginning with our initial concept, and is a guaranteed part of our final website experience.
